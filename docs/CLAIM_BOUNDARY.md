# CLAIM BOUNDARY

## What this repository is

OMNIA-INVARIANCE is an experimental structural analysis framework.

It compares how different systems respond to perturbation, collapse, recovery, instability, saturation, or transformation.

The goal is not semantic understanding.

The goal is structural comparison.

---

## What this repository measures

The framework measures patterns such as:

- stability
- instability
- divergence
- collapse dynamics
- recovery behavior
- perturbation response
- trajectory similarity
- structural invariance
- transition geometry

These measurements are representation-dependent observations produced under explicit transformation rules.

---

## What this repository does NOT claim

This repository does NOT claim:

- a unified theory of reality
- proof of universal physical laws
- proof of consciousness
- proof of causality
- proof of intelligence
- proof of equivalence between domains
- prediction certainty
- semantic understanding
- objective truth extraction

The system does not "understand" systems.

It measures structural behavior.

---

## Important distinction

Structural similarity does NOT imply ontological equivalence.

Example:

Two systems may exhibit similar collapse trajectories while having completely different underlying mechanisms.

The framework only states:

```text
their observable structural responses under selected transformations appear comparable

Nothing more.


---

Boundary of interpretation

All interpretation layers are external.

OMNIA-INVARIANCE only produces measurements.

Meaning attribution, scientific interpretation, philosophical interpretation, or operational decisions belong to external observers.


---

Core principle

measurement != interpretation != decision


---

Scientific posture

This repository should be considered:

exploratory

experimental

falsifiable

incomplete

open to failure

open to refinement


The framework is intended as a structural measurement lens, not as a final explanatory system.


---

Failure condition

If the framework fails to produce stable distinctions across perturbations, domains, or representations, then the framework itself must be considered unstable.

No immunity from failure is assumed.


---

Final note

The repository explores the possibility that some systems may preserve measurable structural relations across transformations.

Whether those relations are useful, meaningful, fundamental, or accidental remains an open question.
