## What is invariance?

Invariance means that a property remains stable under a defined transformation.

General form:

```text
Object or structure: X
Transformation: T
Property: P

P is invariant under T iff:
P(X) = P(T(X))
```

For a family of transformations:

```text
P is invariant under {T_i} iff:
for all i:
  P(X) = P(T_i(X))
```

This is the operational core.

Let:

```text

```

then `P` is invariant under `T`.

## Invariance vs robustness

Invariance and robustness are related but not identical.

Invariance asks:

```text
does the property remain the same?
```

Robustness asks:

```text
does the system remain acceptable despite change?
```

A property may not be perfectly invariant but may still be robust enough for a downstream purpose.

That downstream purpose requires external judgment.

---

## Invariance vs correctness

Correctness is usually semantic, task-specific, or domain-specific.

Invariance is structural.

Therefore:

```text
invariant != correct
unstable != incorrect
```

Correct reading:

```text
invariance provides evidence about structural stability.
```

Incorrect reading:

```text
invariance decides correctness.
```
