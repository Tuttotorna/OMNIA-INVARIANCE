# Stability and Collapse

This document defines the public intuition behind OMNIA-INVARIANCE signal classes.

---

## Stability

Stability means that a structure remains visible, admissible, or compatible under a declared transformation.

Public intuition:

    something survived the transformation

Stability is structurally interesting.

It does not automatically prove semantic truth.

---

## Collapse

Collapse means that a structure fails to survive a declared transformation.

Public intuition:

    something that appeared stable before the transformation did not remain stable after it

Collapse can indicate fragility.

It does not automatically prove semantic falsehood.

---

## Instability

Instability means that structure changes significantly without necessarily fully collapsing.

Public intuition:

    the structure moved or degraded

Instability may justify further measurement or validation.

It is not a final decision.

---

## Candidate invariance

Candidate invariance means that a residue appears stable enough to test further.

Public intuition:

    this may be worth validating

Candidate invariance should be sent to validation, not treated as proof.

---

## Boundary

These signals remain measurement-level or transformation-level signals.

They are not:

- final truth;
- semantic proof;
- downstream decision;
- security finding;
- cryptographic finding.

The boundary remains:

    measurement != inference != decision
