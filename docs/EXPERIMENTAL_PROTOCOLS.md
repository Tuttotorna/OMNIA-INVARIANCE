# OMNIA-INVARIANCE — Experimental Protocols

## Purpose

This document defines the first experimental protocol layer
for evaluating trajectory-space behavior.

The goal is to transform the framework from:

- exploratory geometry

toward:

- repeatable perturbation experiments.

---

# Core Principle

All experiments should be:

- reproducible
- perturbation-controlled
- representation-explicit
- topology-comparable

The framework does not evaluate semantic truth.

It evaluates:

structural perturbation behavior.

---

# Protocol Families

The framework currently defines:

## 1. Raw-Space Protocols

Measure trajectory-space under:

- raw Ω
- raw IRI
- unnormalized trajectories

Purpose:

evaluate combined:

- shape
- magnitude
- energetic behavior

---

## 2. Normalized-Space Protocols

Measure trajectory-space under:

normalized trajectories.

Purpose:

evaluate:

- shape persistence
- topology robustness
- representation stability

---

## 3. Synthetic Stress Protocols

Introduce:

- hybrid trajectories
- oscillatory trajectories
- chaotic trajectories
- noisy perturbation families

Purpose:

stress-test topology coherence.

---

## 4. Transition-Region Protocols

Evaluate systems near:

- cluster boundaries
- hybrid regions
- topology bridges

Purpose:

measure region ambiguity
and structural migration.

---

## 5. Meta-Trajectory Protocols

Evaluate:

trajectory evolution over time.

Purpose:

measure:

- topology drift
- migration
- attractor behavior
- collapse evolution

This layer is currently conceptual.

---

# Standard Experimental Pipeline

A canonical experiment currently follows:

## Step 1

Generate or load trajectories.

---

## Step 2

Construct:

- Ω trajectories
- IRI trajectories

---

## Step 3

Build trajectory-space.

Possible spaces:

- raw
- normalized
- derivative
- volatility
- energy

---

## Step 4

Measure:

- nearest neighbors
- embedding coordinates
- cluster organization
- trajectory classes

---

## Step 5

Evaluate robustness across spaces.

Measure:

- neighbor persistence
- embedding drift
- region stability
- topology survival

---

# Current Experimental Metrics

Examples:

- nearest distance
- embedding displacement
- collapse sharpness
- recovery strength
- volatility
- topology persistence

---

# Current Experimental Boundaries

The framework currently does not claim:

- physical universality
- semantic equivalence
- natural topology laws
- representation independence

All results should currently be interpreted as:

controlled perturbation geometry observations.

---

# Current Methodological Goal

The framework attempts to determine whether:

different systems produce comparable perturbation geometries
under controlled transformations.

The focus is:

not what systems are,

but:

how they structurally react.

---

Experimental protocols are currently:

- exploratory
