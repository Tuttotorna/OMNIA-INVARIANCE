# OMNIA-INVARIANCE — Canonical Benchmark Suite

## Purpose

The framework requires a stable benchmark layer
for evaluating trajectory-space behavior over time.

The goal is to transform experiments from:

- isolated exploratory runs

toward:

- repeatable topology validation.

---

# Core Principle

Benchmarks should validate:

- geometric persistence
- topology robustness
- perturbation consistency
- representation stability

The framework does not benchmark:

- semantic accuracy
- prediction correctness
- task performance

It benchmarks:

structural perturbation geometry.

---

# Benchmark Categories

## 1. Neighbor Persistence Benchmarks

Purpose:

verify whether nearest-neighbor relationships
remain stable across spaces.

Example:

```text
crypto <-> llm

should remain persistent under:

raw-space

normalized-space


Possible interpretation:

stable rigid-collapse geometry.


---

2. Normalization Migration Benchmarks

Purpose:

measure whether systems move under normalization.

Example:

physics

should move closer to:

synthetic_3

after normalization.

Possible interpretation:

shape similarity hidden by magnitude scale.


---

3. Cluster Persistence Benchmarks

Purpose:

verify whether regions survive representation changes.

Example:

rigid-collapse systems should remain regionally coherent after normalization.


---

4. Synthetic Stress Benchmarks

Purpose:

evaluate topology survival under noisy synthetic trajectories.

Examples:

hybrid trajectories

oscillatory trajectories

chaotic trajectories


Questions:

does embedding collapse?

do clusters survive?

do hybrids occupy transition regions?



---

5. Embedding Drift Benchmarks

Purpose:

measure geometric displacement between spaces.

Possible measurements:

raw-space embedding

normalized-space embedding

derivative-space embedding


Questions:

which systems drift most?

which regions remain stable?



---

6. Recovery Geometry Benchmarks

Purpose:

evaluate recovery-dominant structure.

Questions:

does recovery remain identifiable?

does normalization preserve rebound geometry?

do synthetic recovery systems converge?

Failure Conditions

The framework should treat as important failures:

total embedding collapse

random neighbor reassignment

region instability

topology fragmentation

normalization-induced chaos


Failures are informative.

They reveal:

topology fragility

representation dependence

unstable geometric assumptions



---

Current Methodological Importance

Benchmark suites are important because they provide:

reproducibility

longitudinal consistency

framework stability

topology validation

controlled comparison


Without benchmarks, trajectory-space evolution becomes difficult to evaluate.


---

Current Boundary

Benchmarks do not prove:

universal structure

physical laws

semantic equivalence


They only evaluate:

persistence of perturbation geometry under controlled transformations.

Current Status

The Canonical Benchmark Suite is currently:

conceptual

foundational

topology-oriented
