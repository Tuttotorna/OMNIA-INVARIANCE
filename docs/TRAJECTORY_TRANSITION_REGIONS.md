# OMNIA-INVARIANCE — Trajectory Transition Regions

## Core Idea

Trajectory-space may contain not only stable regions,
but also transition regions between structural behaviors.

A system does not necessarily belong to a perfectly isolated class.

Some systems may occupy intermediate geometric positions.

These systems may behave as:

- bridges
- transition layers
- unstable intermediates
- partial hybrids

between major trajectory regions.

# Transition Regions

The framework currently hypothesizes the possible existence of:

## Collapse Basins

Regions dominated by:

- rapid irreversible degradation
- late-stage structural failure
- brittle dynamics

Current examples:

- crypto
- llm

---

## Degradation Regions

Regions dominated by:

- progressive instability
- partial persistence
- moderate perturbation accumulation

Current examples:

- logic
- finance

---

## Recovery Basins

Regions dominated by:

- strong rebound behavior
- persistent global invariants
- high structural elasticity

Current example:

- physics

# Possible Future Structures

With more domains,
trajectory-space may eventually reveal:

- bridges
- attractors
- instability corridors
- transition surfaces
- hybrid zones
- isolated outliers
- structural manifolds

The framework currently does not claim that these structures exist.

It only proposes them as exploratory geometric hypotheses.

---

# Current Methodological Importance

Transition-region analysis is important because:

- real systems are rarely perfectly discrete
- many systems may evolve between behaviors
- geometry may contain continuity rather than rigid categories

This moves the framework from:

- static classification

toward:

- continuous structural topology

---

# Important Boundary

Current transition regions are exploratory.

They depend on:

- current trajectory definitions
- perturbation construction
- embedding methods
- distance metrics
- available domains

The framework does not currently prove:

- natural trajectory topology
- universal transition geometry
- existence of structural manifolds

Further validation requires:

- more domains
- denser trajectory populations
- repeated perturbation families
- temporal trajectory evolution
- statistical topology analysis

---

# Current Status

Transition-region analysis is currently a conceptual extension layer.

It should be interpreted as:

- exploratory topology
- geometric hypothesis generation
- perturbation-response analysis

not as finalized scientific structure.
