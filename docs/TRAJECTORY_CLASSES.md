# Trajectory Classes

## Purpose

This document defines the preliminary trajectory classes observed in OMNIA-INVARIANCE.

The goal is not to define universal categories.

The goal is to describe recurring structural behaviors observed across domains under controlled perturbation.

These classes remain experimental.

---

# Core Principle

Different systems may exhibit different structural responses under perturbation even when measured through the same invariance framework.

Trajectory classes describe these response patterns.

The classification operates on:

- Ω trajectories
- IRI trajectories
- collapse dynamics
- recovery behavior
- perturbation response

---

# Current Preliminary Classes

## 1. Rigid-Collapse Trajectory

Observed domains:

- crypto
- llm

### Characteristics

```text
high initial coherence
slow early degradation
threshold-sensitive collapse
rapid irreversibility growth
```

### Typical Shape

Ω:

```text
high -> moderate -> sharp decline
```

IRI:

```text
low -> increasing -> rapid escalation
```

### Interpretation

These systems appear structurally rigid during early perturbation stages.

However, once structural thresholds are crossed, degradation accelerates rapidly.

This produces collapse-dominant behavior.

---

## 2. Smooth-Degradation Trajectory

Observed domains:

- logic

### Characteristics

```text
moderate coherence persistence
gradual degradation
limited collapse acceleration
stable irreversibility growth
```

### Typical Shape

Ω:

```text
moderate decline with partial persistence
```

IRI:

```text
slow and stable increase
```

### Interpretation

These systems degrade progressively without abrupt collapse regions.

Structural weakening appears smoother and more distributed.

---

## 3. Recovery-Dominant Trajectory

Observed domains:

- physics

### Characteristics

```text
high early instability
strong coherence recovery
global structural persistence
irreversibility normalization
```

### Typical Shape

Ω:

```text
initial collapse -> recovery -> stabilization
```

IRI:

```text
high spike -> rapid reduction
```

### Interpretation

These systems appear capable of recovering global structural coherence after early perturbation instability.

The trajectory suggests resilience or conserved global structure.

---

# Important Boundary

These classes are observational.

They do not yet define universal laws.

They may depend on:

- perturbation design
- scaling
- normalization
- domain construction
- trajectory resolution

Further validation is required.

---

# Research Direction

Future work may investigate:

- whether additional domains fall into existing classes
- whether hybrid trajectories exist
- whether trajectory transitions occur
- whether class boundaries are measurable
- whether trajectory classes correlate with robustness or fragility

---

# Current Operational Value

Trajectory classes currently provide:

- visual interpretation support
- cross-domain comparison language
- structural behavior categorization
- perturbation-response taxonomy

They help describe how systems respond structurally under transformation.

---

# Summary

OMNIA-INVARIANCE currently identifies three preliminary trajectory classes:

- rigid-collapse
- smooth-degradation
- recovery-dominant

These classes describe different structural stability behaviors under controlled perturbation.

The classification remains experimental and subject to future validation.
