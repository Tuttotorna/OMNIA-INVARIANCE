# OMNIA-INVARIANCE — Trajectory Space Regions

## Core Idea

Trajectory-space appears to organize into regions defined by
structural response under perturbation.

The framework does not classify systems by ontology.

Instead, it classifies systems by trajectory behavior.

---

# Current Observed Regions

## 1. Rigid-Collapse Region

### Observed domains

- crypto
- llm

### Core behavior

Systems remain highly coherent initially,
then collapse rapidly after perturbation accumulation.

### Typical properties

- high initial Ω
- strong late-stage Ω drop
- positive IRI growth
- minimal recovery
- low structural elasticity

### Current geometric signals

- nearest-neighbor pairing
- tight embedding proximity
- low pairwise trajectory distance

### Structural interpretation

These systems behave like brittle structures.

They preserve apparent stability until collapse becomes unavoidable.

---

## 2. Smooth-Degradation Region

### Observed domains

- finance
- logic

### Core behavior

Systems degrade progressively rather than collapsing abruptly.

Partial structure remains visible after instability.

### Typical properties

- moderate Ω decline
- moderate IRI growth
- smoother volatility profile
- incomplete degradation
- partial persistence

### Current geometric signals

- intermediate embedding position
- mutual nearest-neighbor relationship
- medium pairwise trajectory distance

### Structural interpretation

These systems behave like gradual erosion processes.

Perturbation accumulates continuously rather than catastrophically.

---

## 3. Recovery-Dominant Region

### Observed domains

- physics

### Core behavior

Systems exhibit strong instability followed by structural recovery.

Global organization survives despite local perturbation.

### Typical properties

- early Ω instability
- strong recovery strength
- negative IRI growth rate
- high volatility
- persistent global invariants

### Current geometric signals

- embedding outlier
- large pairwise distances
- isolated clustering behavior

### Structural interpretation

These systems behave like elastic or self-stabilizing structures.

Local instability does not necessarily imply global collapse.

---

# Emerging Geometry

The current trajectory-space suggests the possible emergence of:

- collapse basins
- degradation regions
- recovery basins
- transition zones

The framework currently does not claim:

- universal topology
- complete trajectory taxonomy
- domain-independent structural laws

The current regions are empirical observations generated from:

- trajectory classification
- morphometrics
- pairwise distances
- clustering
- embedding analysis

---

# Current Working Hypothesis

Trajectory-space may organize according to
structural response modes rather than semantic domain identity.

Under this hypothesis:

- systems from unrelated domains may occupy nearby regions
- structurally similar collapse dynamics may generate geometric proximity
- ontology becomes secondary to perturbation behavior

This remains a methodological hypothesis only.

Further validation requires:

- more domains
- larger datasets
- repeated perturbation families
- alternative embeddings
- statistical testing
- independent replication

---

# Current Status

Trajectory-space regions are currently exploratory.

They should be interpreted as:

- geometric observations
- structural groupings
- perturbation-response categories

not as finalized scientific classes.
